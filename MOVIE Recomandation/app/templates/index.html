ML Pipeline — 9 Steps
01

Data Ingestion

10,000 TMDB top-rated movies. Key columns: vote_average, vote_count, popularity, release_date, original_language, overview.

pandasCSV
02

Feature Engineering (9 features)

Log-scale popularity & vote_count (fix skew). Compute movie_age, is_english. Encode language (top-5+other). Derive overview_length, word_count, pop×votes, age×votes.

numpylog1pLabelEncoderinteraction terms
03

Target Binning

vote_average → 3-class tier: Average (5.4–6.5), Good (6.5–7.5), Excellent (7.5+). Classification instead of regression.

pd.cut3-class
04

Train / Test Split & Scaling

80/20 stratified split. StandardScaler fitted on train only (no data leakage). All 9 features normalised to μ=0, σ=1.

train_test_splitStandardScalerstratify
05

9 Baseline Models

LR, DT, RF, Extra Trees, Gradient Boosting, AdaBoost, SVM (RBF), Naive Bayes, KNN — all evaluated on Accuracy, F1, Precision, Recall, MCC.

scikit-learncross_val_scoreStratifiedKFold
06

XGBoost & LightGBM

State-of-the-art gradient-boosted trees. XGBoost uses mlogloss eval; LightGBM uses leaf-wise growth with early stopping on validation set.

xgboostlightgbmearly stopping
07

Hyperparameter Tuning — Optuna

Bayesian optimisation with TPE sampler. Three independent studies (RF, XGBoost, LightGBM) — 12–20 trials each, maximising 3-fold CV accuracy. Improvements of ~1–2% over baseline.

optunaTPEBayesian
08

Soft Voting Ensemble

RF Tuned + Gradient Boosting + SVM combined via soft voting — averages class probabilities for more robust, stable predictions.

VotingClassifiersoft votingprobability avg
09

Pickle Persistence & Serving

All 14 models + scaler + label encoder saved as .pkl files. Flask loads everything once at startup — sub-millisecond inference, zero retraining per request.

pickleFlaskREST API
